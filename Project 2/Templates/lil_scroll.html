Baby Names Through the Decades

1880s

John has stayed a consistently popular name for boys. It had a long run at number 1 for many decades and continue to be in the top 10. One in every 25 Americans is named John.

Mary has been one of the most popular girl names with over 3.8 million babies have been named Mary. It was the number 1 or 2 girl name from the beginning of record keeping until 1965, when it started to slide.

1890s

The most popular boy name is James. More than 4.8 million boys have been named James , nearly 3 percent of all boys born. It was number 1 from 1940-1952.

Margaret was far more popular in the earlier days of record keeping but its decline in popularity has been very slow and gradual, meaning that although it hasn’t made the top 10 for decades, but manages to be the 10th girl name overall. Over 1 million baby girls have been named Margaret.

1900s

William is another steady classic name, like James, and John, that has maintained its consistent level of popularity. It did have a brief, minor dip in the 1980s and '90s, but it has stayed in the top 10 and 20 over the decades.

The name Helen reached its most popular peak in the early 1900’s when the greatest number of babies was given the name in 1918. There have been only about 721K girls name Helen since 1880.

1910s

Like James and John, Robert has stayed consistently popular over the years. It managed to stay in the top ten until the end of the 1980s. In 1934 Robert, and its variations of Bobby and Bob, all made the top 100.

Dorothy is the 15th most popular name where approximately 1 million girls have the name. It reached its peak in 1924 when about 40 thousand babies were given the name. Those people are now 93 years old.

1920s

Charles ranks 10th of all boy names, with over 2 million totals. It has maintained a steady general popularity but hit its peak in 1929.

The name Elizabeth was having its heyday in the early 1900s, it has stayed consistent over the years, resisting and weathering trends, neither spiking nor dropping off in popularity. It’s the 5th most popular name.

1930s

Richard is the 7th most popular name and stayed in the top 10 until the 1970’s. It had its biggest year in 1947.

After Mary, the second most popular name for girls is Patricia. Though it never made number 1 for any particular year, it stayed close to it through the baby boom years, from 1946-1964.

1940s

David has been another steady classic, but it only reached number 1 once, in 1960. But that was a big year for births, so almost 86,000 babies got the name David in that year alone.

The window of popularity for Linda was relatively brief, although it’s the 4th most popular name. It was mostly confined to the baby boom years. It hit number 1 in 1947, after a Buddy Clark song, "Linda," topped the charts.

1950s

There were more than 450,000 baby boys named Thomas in the 1950s, helping to make it the 9th most popular name. Although it managed to stay in the top 20 over the decades, its popularity began to decline in the 1970s.

In the early Hollywood film era, glamorous actresses like Barbara La Marr, Barbara Bedford, Barbara Kent, and Barbara Stanwyck gave the name Barbara a boost. It stayed in the top 10 from 1927-1958 but dropped off quickly after that.

1960s

Michael is the most common boy name for people currently alive. It has not been out of the top 10 names given to baby boys since 1943 and had an unbroken streak at number 1 from 1961 to 1998.

Susan was a typical baby boom name and is the 7th most popular girl name. At its peak in 1960, over 39,000 baby girls were named Susan.

1970s

Christopher is the 11th most popular boy name. Over 2.1 million boys have the name and reached it peaks in the early 1980s.

Jennifer was the number 1 girl name post the baby boom. and it stayed in that position from 1970 until 1984, the year of its peak popularity. It probably got its long-term boost from the 1970 film Love Story, starring Ali MacGraw as a beautiful, tragic character bearing the name.

1980s

Joshua is the 22nd most popular name, but it was neither consistently popular over the whole time, nor a baby boomer name. Joshua didn’t break the top 20 until the 1980s, and it achieved peak popularity in 2006.

Jessica’s rise to the 8th most popular name started a little after the Jennifer craze began, but it was probably bolstered by Jennifer and other popular J names like Jason and Joshua. It stayed in the top 10 through the 1980s and '90s.

1990s

Matthew has become a classic boy name first becoming popular in the 1970s ranking in the top 25 for baby names and 13th overall totals. With its religious meaning, “gift of God”, the name has widespread use across several cultures.

Ashley was traditionally a boy name, notably as the name of Scarlett O'Hara’s love interest in the hugely popular novel and film Gone with the Wind. It got a big boost as a girl's name in the early 1980s, when it was the name of a female character on the soap opera The Young and the Restless.

2000s

Jacob was a rather old-fashioned sounding name when it cracked the top 100 in the mid-1970s, but after a 14-year run as the number 1 baby name for boys starting in 1999, it established itself as the name of a new generation.

Emily spent over a decade as the number 1 girl name, from 1996-2007. it’s the 20th most popular name, with over 813,000 girls given the name.

2010s

Noah is the current number 1 boy name so far this decade, and though it only broke the top 100 starting in 1995, it already ranks 82nd as the most popular of all time. It's part of a newer trend toward biblical names ending in a vowel sound, like Elijah, Jonah, and Isaiah.

Emma is the current number 1 name for girls in this decade and the 49th most popular girl name. It was popular in the year 1900, then dropped off until its rise to the top 20 in 1999. Names go in and out of style, but Emma proves that they can go out and come back after a long absence.
